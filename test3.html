The North American Video Game Crash (1983)

Three available video game consoles during the North American Video Game Crash in 1983 were the Nintendo Entertainment System, the Atari 400 and 800 computes, and Sinclair Research ZX81 microcomputers.

The Rise of Games in the East (1985-1995)

Sega released its 16-bit Genesis console in North America as a successor to its 1986 Sega Master System, which failed to compete against the NES.In the mid-1990s, video games leaped to the Big Screen with the release of the Super Mario Bros. In 1995, Sega released in North America its Saturn system, the first 32-bit console that played games on CDs.
